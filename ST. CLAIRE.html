SAINT CLAIRE OF ASSISI

     
St. Clare of Assisi (1194-1253) was an Italian saint who

left a wealthy family to live a life of poverty and prayer.

She was the co-founder of the Order of Poor Ladies, also

known as the Clares.


Clare of Assisi was born into the nobility of Assisi on July 16, 1194.

She was known by members of her household to be a sensitive child,

prayerful and kind. After hearing St. Francis preach, she confided

in him her desire to live for God.
